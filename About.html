Methodology

Collection Completion:

Any new cards that are duplicates will be disenchanted.
This will be done without any deference to whether a new card (or old card) is golden.
Example: Unwrapping a Golden Rag followed by a regular Rag will disenchant the regular one, yielding 400 dust. Unwrapping them in the reverse order will yield 1600 dust.
Because of this assumption, and where it may differ from your actual behavior, the simulator may slightly diverge from your actual experience.
If you would always keep the Golden Ragnaros (in the example above) the simulator will under-estimate your needed cards.
If you would always keep the normal card, and DE the golden, the simulator will over-estimate.

Hearthstone Assumptions:

Cards are selected in a two-roll system
The first roll determines quality, based on the following chances:
Legendary: 1.19%
Epic: 4.76%
Rare: 23.8%
Common: 70.25%
The second roll determines if the card is Golden. For Rare, Epic, and Legendary cards this is a 5% chance, for common it is 2%.

Optimal Pack Choice:

The Optimal Pack is determined by computing the expected value (in dust) of each of the three pack options.

EV is Expected Value
V is Value. Value of a new card is the cost to craft that card. Value of a duplicate card is the average dust of that level card (taking into account that some are golden cards).
P is Probability
Subscripts (C/R/E/L) are rarity (Common/Rare/Epic/Legendary)
So we get the expected dust value of a single card, and compare sets vs. each other. All V variables will be the same between sets, but based on how complete one set or another is, the probability of a new or duplicate will vary (and vary by card rarity).
It may be easier to think of this in terms of requried dust to finish a set, and the EV is how much that required dust will be, on average, reduced.
